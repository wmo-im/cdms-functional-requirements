== Terms and definitions

For the purposes of this document, the terms and definitions given in WMO-No. 1131 and the following apply:

Availability::
proportion of time a system or service is operational and accessible to users, typically expressed as a
percentage over a defined measurement period
+
[NOTE]
====
In this specification, availability refers to data retrieval and ingestion services. Not to be confused
with data availability, which refers to the presence or absence of observation records in the archive.
====

Break point::
a point in time at which a statistically significant discontinuity is detected in a climate observation
time series, attributable to a non-climatic cause such as station relocation, instrument change, or
change in observation practice
+
[NOTE]
====
Break points are identified during the homogenisation process. Each break point separates segments of
a time series within which different adjustment factors may be applied.
====

Climate normals::
standard reference values representing the average state of a climate variable over a defined reference
period, computed in accordance with WMO guidelines (WMO-No. 1203). Standard reference periods are
30 consecutive years (currently 1991–2020)

Data Collection and Processing System (DCPS)::
a national system responsible for the real-time collection, initial validation, and transmission of
observational data from field sensors and observing networks to downstream data management systems
+
[NOTE]
====
The DCPS typically applies real-time quality checks appropriate for operational meteorology. The primary
integration point addressed by this specification is the delivery of data from the DCPS to the Climate
Data Management System for long-term quality assurance and archival. The internal operation of the DCPS
is outside the scope of this specification.
====

Data lineage::
a summary record of the processing history of a dataset as a whole, documenting the sequence of
operations applied from source data through to the current published state, structured following the
W3C PROV Data Model. Distinct from observation-level provenance metadata, which records the immutable
processing history of individual observations

Data rescue::
the process of recovering historical climate observations from non-digital sources — primarily paper
records, registers, and ledgers — by transcribing the data into machine-readable form, with appropriate
quality assurance, and integrating the rescued observations into the observational record with complete
provenance
+
[NOTE]
====
Automated digitization via optical character recognition (OCR) is outside the scope of this specification.
====

Data stewardship::
the assignment to an entity of defined rights and responsibilities for acquiring, managing, maintaining,
and ensuring the long-term accessibility and integrity of climate data, encompassing quality assurance,
lifecycle management, access control, and accountability for the ongoing fitness of the data record.
Adapted from WMO-No. 1238

Derived observation::
an observation value computed by the system from one or more source observations by means of a documented
formula, aggregation, or statistical procedure, as distinct from a value directly reported by a measuring
instrument. Examples include computed meteorological variables (e.g. mean sea level pressure derived from
station pressure), statistical aggregates (e.g. daily maximum temperature), and homogenised values

Discovery metadata::
dataset-level metadata records that make data findable in a discovery catalogue, describing what data
exists, where it can be accessed, its spatial and temporal extent, responsible organisation, and data
policy classification
+
[NOTE]
====
Distinct from station and sensor metadata, which describe the observing platform and instruments that
produced the data.
====

Double-key entry::
a data entry quality assurance procedure in which two operators independently transcribe the same source
document, after which the two transcriptions are compared automatically and any discrepancies are flagged
for resolution before the values are accepted into the observational record

Earth system domains::
the atmospheric, terrestrial, hydrological, cryospheric, and oceanic environments from which in-situ
point observations relevant to climate are collected

Fitness-for-purpose::
an assessment of whether a dataset meets the quality and completeness requirements of a particular
intended application or use case, summarising quality metrics — including completeness, temporal
continuity, quality control flag distribution, and known systematic issues — in terms of suitability
for defined use cases such as climatological trend analysis, climate normals computation, or extreme
event studies

Homogenisation::
the process of detecting and correcting non-climatic discontinuities in long-term observation time series
caused by changes in station location, instrumentation, observation practices, or surrounding environment,
in order to produce a temporally consistent series suitable for climate trend analysis. Results in an
adjusted series stored alongside the original, unadjusted series with full provenance documentation

Inhomogeneity::
a discontinuity or step-change in a climate time series attributable to non-climatic factors such as
station relocation, instrument replacement, changes in observation practices, or changes to the station
environment, rather than to actual changes in the observed climate variable

In-situ point observation::
an observation obtained by direct measurement at a specific geographic location, representing conditions
at that point in space
+
[NOTE]
====
Encompasses measurements from fixed stations, where geographic location is derived from station metadata,
and from mobile platforms such as ships, buoys, radiosondes, and aircraft, where geographic location is
stored with each individual observation. Excludes gridded, raster, reanalysis, satellite-derived, radar
composite, and forecast data products. The scope of this specification is restricted to in-situ point
observations.
====

Normalisation::
the process of transforming observational data from diverse source formats, encodings, and unit systems
into a common internal representation, resolving differences in format conventions, units of measure,
coordinate systems, timestamp formats, and character encodings
+
[NOTE]
====
Not to be confused with statistical normalisation, which scales data to a standard range or distribution.
====

Observable element::
a uniquely defined measurable quantity characterised by its measurement class (e.g. temperature,
precipitation, pressure), accumulation or averaging duration, statistical function (instantaneous,
mean, maximum, minimum, accumulated total), and unit of measure
+
[NOTE]
====
Two measurements that differ in any of these properties constitute distinct elements and shall be stored
and described separately to ensure unambiguous interpretation across stations and datasets. Source data
provenance (e.g. transmission format or originating network) is recorded separately rather than embedded
in the element definition.
====

Provenance metadata::
information documenting the origin, processing history, and all modifications applied to an observation
or dataset throughout its lifecycle, from initial acquisition through archival and distribution. For
individual observations, provenance includes source identification, ingestion details, quality control
decisions, manual corrections, homogenisation adjustments, and data rescue workflows. Adapted from
WMO-No. 1238

Quality flag::
a coded indicator attached to an observation record documenting the outcome of one or more quality
control checks applied to that observation, recording whether a value is accepted, suspect, or rejected,
and indicating the specific check that assigned the flag and the level at which it was applied
(automatic, manual, or final)
